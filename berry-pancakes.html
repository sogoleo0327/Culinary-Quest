Berry Pancakes

Fluffy pancakes loaded with fresh berries and a touch of honey

Prep Time
10 minutes
Cook Time
15 minutes
Servings
4 people
Difficulty
Medium

Pancake Batter Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups buttermilk (or milk with 1 tbsp lemon juice)
  • 1/4 cup melted butter or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed fresh berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • Butter or oil for cooking
  • Maple syrup, honey, or powdered sugar for serving

How to Make Pancake Batter & Cook

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Mix wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, buttermilk, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Combine mixtures: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined. Don't overmix - a few lumps are okay!
  4. Add berries: Gently fold in the fresh berries, being careful not to crush them.
  5. Let batter rest: Allow the batter to rest for 5-10 minutes while you heat your griddle or pan.
  6. Heat cooking surface: Heat a griddle or large non-stick pan over medium heat. Add a small amount of butter or oil.
  7. Cook pancakes: Pour 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake onto the hot surface. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes.
  8. Flip and finish: Carefully flip the pancakes and cook for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown on both sides.
  9. Keep warm: Transfer cooked pancakes to a warm oven (200°F) while you cook the remaining batter.
  10. Serve: Stack the pancakes and serve hot with maple syrup, honey, or powdered sugar and extra fresh berries!

Tips & Variations

  • For extra fluffy pancakes, separate the eggs and fold in whipped egg whites at the end.
  • If you don't have buttermilk, mix 1 tablespoon lemon juice with 1 cup milk and let sit for 5 minutes.
  • Try different berry combinations: blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, or blackberries.
  • Add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the batter for extra flavor.
  • For chocolate chip pancakes, replace berries with chocolate chips.
  • Make the batter the night before and refrigerate for even fluffier pancakes in the morning.
