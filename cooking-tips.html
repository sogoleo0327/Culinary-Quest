Cooking Tips

How to Mince an Onion

  1. Cut the onion in half from root to tip and peel off the skin.
  2. Place the flat side down and make horizontal cuts toward the root, but don’t cut all the way through.
  3. Make vertical cuts from top to bottom, again leaving the root intact.
  4. Slice across the onion to create small, even pieces. Discard the root end.

How to Hold a Chef’s Knife

  1. Grip the handle with your three back fingers.
  2. Pinch the blade just in front of the handle with your thumb and index finger for control.
  3. Keep your wrist straight and use a rocking motion for safe, efficient cutting.

How to Butter Baste

  1. Heat your pan and cook your protein (like steak or chicken) until nearly done.
  2. Add a generous knob of butter, along with aromatics like garlic and herbs.
  3. Tilt the pan and use a spoon to continuously scoop and pour the melted butter over the food for extra flavor and moisture.

How to Flambé

  1. Add alcohol (like brandy or rum) to your hot pan with food.
  2. Carefully ignite the alcohol with a long lighter or match.
  3. Let the flames burn off naturally, then continue cooking as desired.

How to Make Vanilla Extract

  1. Split vanilla beans lengthwise to expose the seeds.
  2. Place the beans in a clean glass jar and cover with vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for at least 2 months, shaking occasionally.

How to Scramble an Egg

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl and whisk with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a nonstick pan over medium-low heat and add butter.
  3. Pour in the eggs and gently stir with a spatula until just set and creamy.

How to Store Fresh Herbs

  1. Trim the stems and place herbs in a glass of water like a bouquet.
  2. Cover loosely with a plastic bag and refrigerate.
  3. Change the water every couple of days to keep herbs fresh longer.
